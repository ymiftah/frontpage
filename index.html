About me

Education

I graduated from ENSTA Paris, a very respectable engineering school in France (and actually the oldest !).

As a généraliste engineer, I was trained in a wide range of topics, with however a strong focus un mechanical engineering, and Computational Fluid Dynamics in particular. I thus had two research experiences in Japan first at Kyushu University, then in France at CEA, performing CFD simulations.

Following an exchange at the Technical University Munich my interest shifted from mechanical design to energy management, and I majored in my final year at ENSTA in Energy Production and Energy Management.

Obviously I didn't stop learning after graduation, and I keep building new skills wherever curiosity carries me !

Professional Experience

I have been working for three years at Engie, a global player in the energy and utilities industry, and a company commited to be leader in the energy transition.

I participated in various energy systems optimization projects, leveraging on computational power and machine intelligence to operate energy systems in an efficient manner. At the crossroads of traditionnal mathematical programming and data science, I worked in the development of algorithms to save energy in District Energy Systems, Microgrids, or HVAC systems.
